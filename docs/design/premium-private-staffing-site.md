# GoodPeople Premium Private Staffing Site

## Goal

Turn the public site into a premium international staffing experience for wealthy private clients, founders, business owners, family offices, assistants, and representatives of families who can afford high-end domestic and lifestyle staff.

The site must feel more confidential, composed, global, and trustworthy than a standard nanny agency. It should explain what GoodPeople can solve, why the company is safe to work with, how selection works, where the agency can search, and how to start a discreet brief.

## Reference Audit

### English Nanny

- Strong international signal: Russian, English, Chinese, Kazakh versions; Moscow office and global audience.
- Deep service catalog: language nannies, newborn nannies, tutors, teachers, coaches, housekeepers, chefs, drivers, bodyguards, estate managers, assistants, butlers, carers, VIP wardrobe specialists, visas, translation, education support.
- Trust content: client reviews, press, nanny feedback, children's achievements, process of education, candidate checks, FAQ, commercial terms.
- Candidate vetting ideas: criminal history, references, documents, interviews, medical certificates, first aid, education diplomas, language/teaching qualification.
- Takeaway: GoodPeople should show not only "we recruit nannies", but a full private-life support system with verification and expert content.

### KD Concierge

- Strong premium positioning for demanding clients.
- Important differentiation: training for household staff, chefs, drivers, assistants, VIP wardrobe specialists, nannies, and housekeepers.
- The premium client buys not only a candidate, but a service standard.
- Takeaway: GoodPeople should present a "private staff standard": etiquette, security, confidentiality, travel readiness, communication, household process discipline.

### Mi-Mi Agency

- Strong clarity in process: meeting the client, approving the brief, recruiting, organizing introductions, trial days, support and replacement.
- Human reassurance: personal managers, quality over quantity, comfortable office, brand reputation.
- Takeaway: keep the process visible and easy. Wealthy clients delegate because they want fewer decisions, not more forms.

### Home Staff International

- Strong global architecture: local and relocation-ready candidates, offices/network, fast WhatsApp/Telegram response, vacancy examples with geography and salary.
- Service categories are broader than "home": childcare, household care, culinary staff, management and assistants, professional crew, elderly care.
- Expert content: news and articles about salaries, hiring in Dubai, country-specific rules and expectations.
- Takeaway: GoodPeople should show geography, offices/representatives, salary-market intelligence, open examples, and articles to prove expertise.

## Audience

- Founders, investors, executives, private entrepreneurs.
- Families with Moscow, Dubai, Europe, UK, or seasonal residences.
- Family office, personal assistants, lifestyle managers, estate managers.
- Clients who value privacy, safety, speed, continuity, and cultural fit over the lowest commission.

## Positioning

GoodPeople is a confidential private staffing office for families and business owners. It recruits domestic, childcare, lifestyle, executive, and residence staff for Moscow and international lifestyles.

Core message:

> We find people who can be trusted with the home, children, privacy, property, schedule, and family rhythm.

## Site Structure

1. Hero: international confidential private staffing for families and business owners.
2. Trust rail: response speed, vetted shortlist, NDA-first, worldwide search.
3. Who we recruit: grouped service catalog with all major roles.
4. Why choose us: selection standards, references, security, quality over quantity, personal manager.
5. Process: 6-step client journey from confidential brief to aftercare.
6. Office map and geography: Moscow base plus Dubai, London, Monaco, Geneva, Paris, Milan, Cyprus, New York, relocation-ready search.
7. Standards and checks: documents, references, security screen, medical/first aid, trial days, adaptation.
8. Articles and market intelligence: salary guides, country-specific hiring, nanny/governess differences, Dubai/Europe hiring, staff contracts, confidentiality.
9. Example briefs/vacancies: show level of roles and salary ranges without exposing client identity.
10. Contact and brief form: low-friction confidential request.

## Visual Direction

- Premium editorial, not a generic card-heavy agency page.
- Background: deep graphite, ivory, warm white, restrained gold, muted sage.
- Typography: large calm hero, clean Russian body copy, compact labels.
- Imagery: private residence, warm household service, world-map/office motif, not stock smiles.
- Components: open sections, thin borders, editorial lists, map panel, process rows, compact role matrix.
- Motion: subtle hover lift, no flashy animations.
- Trust tone: quiet authority, confidentiality, international competence.

## Required Content Ideas

- "Кого подбираем": nannies, governesses, tutors, newborn care, housekeepers, chefs, family drivers, security, assistants, estate managers, butlers, gardeners, carers, wardrobe specialists, domestic couples, seasonal villa teams, relocation-ready staff.
- "Как проверяем": interview, documents, references, security screening, medical/first aid where relevant, education and certificates, trial day, adaptation control.
- "Карта офисов": Moscow office, international search points, city chips and visual map.
- "Статьи": market insights that answer expensive-client questions before the call.
- "Примеры запросов": confidential examples with geography and compensation range to prove GoodPeople understands premium assignments.
- "Почему с нами": quality over quantity, curated shortlist, personal manager, replacement/aftercare, privacy, speed, international search.

## UX Principles

- The first viewport must immediately say: global, confidential, premium, private staff.
- Clients should never feel they are entering a mass job board.
- Every section should answer a buying objection: trust, privacy, speed, quality, geography, expertise, price level, replacement, process.
- The page should have enough detail for an assistant/family office to evaluate GoodPeople before sending a private brief.
